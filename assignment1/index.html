/03. PRIMARY GOAL

[Q. 05] What is primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal of the interactive experience appears to be showcasing Rich Brown’s design expertise and portfolio to attract potential clients and collaborators. This objective is clearly communicated through the prominent display of projects, awards, and client testimonials.

The site’s structure and visual emphasis on these elements highlight Rich Brown’s professional achievements and creative work, effectively positioning him as a skilled and accomplished designer. The overall experience is designed to leave a strong, positive impression on visitors and encourage further engagement.

[Q. 06] How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The interactive experience effectively communicates the primary goal by employing a clean design and professional layout, which reinforce the credibility and expertise of the presenter.

All information is illustrated with high-quality images and videos, ensuring that the content is both engaging and informative. The user-friendly interface and simple navigation facilitate an intuitive user experience, making it easy for visitors to find and interact with the material.

The contact information button is always pinned on the page, ensuring that users can easily reach out for further inquiries or collaborations.

/04. OVER TIME

[Q. 07] What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

The experience is designed to accommodate both brief visits and extended explorations, depending on the user's interest.

For quick visits, Each project title is accompanied by illustrative images, allowing users to swiftly grasp the essence of each project.

For those seeking more in-depth information, The website offers project pages that include an overview, videos, awards, and reflections. These pages also feature hyperlinks to client sites, enabling users to interact directly and gain a more comprehensive understanding of the projects undertaken by Rich Brown.

While the website is structured to engage users for extended periods, it is not necessarily designed to encourage frequent revisits.

[Q. 08] How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The interactive experience of the website suggests that users can return multiple times to explore Rich Brown's work, as the structure provides easily accessible sections and detailed project pages. The site showcases 22 projects spanning from 2017 to the present, with recent and most significant projects featured prominently, while past projects are listed in the archive to demonstrate his growth over time.

However, repeated visits might be limited since the site primarily serves as a portfolio, with new information added only when new projects are completed.

/05. OTHER MEDIA FORMS

[Q. 09] What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The Richbrown portfolio website evokes similarities with the Behance platform, a well-known showcase site for designers.

Behance's main page displays a grid of thumbnail images representing various artworks, encouraging users to click on individual pieces for detailed information. Each designer has a dedicated page where they upload their work and provide contact information for potential hiring or collaboration. For detailed project information, designers on Behance can arrange images, GIFs, videos, descriptions, and external links freely to illustrate and explain their projects.

Similarly, Richbrown's portfolio website reflects this design and functional approach. It functions like a miniaturized version of Behance, focusing more on presenting the creator’s work and personal brand.
